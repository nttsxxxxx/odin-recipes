Chocolate Chip Cookies

Chocolate chip cookies! Sources say they began in 1938 when Ruth Graves Wakefield chopped up a Nestlé chocolate bar and added it to a cookie recipe. Earlier cookie recipes exist, but hers became the famous one.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup softened butter
  • 1 cup white sugar
  • 1 cup packed brown sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 2 teaspoons vanilla extract
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • 2 teaspoons hot water
  • ½ teaspoon salt
  • 3 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 cups chocolate chips
  • 1 cup chopped walnuts

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. Beat the butter and both sugars until smooth and creamy.
  3. Beat in the eggs one at a time, then stir in the vanilla.
  4. Dissolve the baking soda in hot water and add it to the batter. Add salt and mix.
  5. Stir in the flour, chocolate chips, and walnuts to form a soft dough.
  6. Roll into balls and place ~2 inches apart on ungreased baking sheets.
  7. Bake until lightly browned, about 10 minutes.
  8. Cool on the sheets for 1–2 minutes before transferring to a wire rack.
  9. Let cool completely, then enjoy!
