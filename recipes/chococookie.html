Chocolate Chip Cookies

Chocolate chip cookies! Sources say it started in 1938 when Ruth Graves Wakefield chopped up a Nestle chocolate bar and added it to a cookie recipe. Other recipes earlier that this exist, but she was chosen for whatever reason.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup of softened butter
  • 1 cup of white sugar
  • 1 cup of packed brown sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 2 teaspoons of vanilla extract
  • 1 teaspoon of baking soda
  • 2 teaspoons of hot water
  • half a teaspoon of salt
  • 3 cups of all purpose flour
  • 2 cups of chocolate chips
  • 1 cup of chopped walnuts

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 350°F, or 175°C
  2. With an electric mixer, Beat the butter and both sugars in a large bowl until its smooth and creamy
  3. Beat in the eggs one at a time, then stir in the vanilla
  4. Dissolve the baking soda in hot water and add to the batter, then add the salt and mix
  5. Stir in the flour, chocolate chips, and walnuts until its a soft dough.
  6. Make into balls and put them ~2 inches apart onto ungreased baking sheets
  7. Bake until lightly browned (~10 minutes).
  8. Cool on the sheets for a minute or two before moving to a wire rack to cool.
  9. Finish cooling, and enjoy!.
